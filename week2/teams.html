Current Missions

Explore the ongoing missions conducted by NASA, contributing to our understanding of the universe and advancing scientific knowledge. Below is a selection of current NASA missions:

Mission Name Objective Launch Date
Perseverance Rover (Mars) Search for signs of past life and collect Martian soil samples. July 30, 2020
James Webb Space Telescope Observe the universe in infrared wavelengths, studying the formation of galaxies, stars, and planets. Planned Launch: December 18, 2021
Artemis Program Return humans to the Moon and prepare for future crewed missions to Mars. Ongoing
Europa Clipper Study Jupiter's moon Europa to assess its habitability and potential for life. Planned Launch: Mid-2020s
Dragonfly (Titan) Explore Saturn's moon Titan with a rotorcraft to study prebiotic chemistry and extraterrestrial habitability. Planned Launch: 2027

These missions represent NASA's commitment to pushing the boundaries of exploration and expanding our understanding of the cosmos.
