Background

Space exploration has been a fascinating journey since the mid-20th century. The first human-made object to reach outer space was the V-2 rocket in 1944. The space race between the United States and the Soviet Union led to significant milestones, including Yuri Gagarin's orbit of Earth in 1961.

NASA, the National Aeronautics and Space Administration, was established in 1958 and has been at the forefront of space exploration efforts. Over the years, various missions and projects have expanded our understanding of the universe.

Space exploration involves sending spacecraft, satellites, and humans beyond Earth's atmosphere to explore celestial bodies, conduct research, and gain insights into the cosmos.
